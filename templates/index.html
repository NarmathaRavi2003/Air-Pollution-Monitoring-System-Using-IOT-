Did You Know?

Air pollution is a major environmental risk to health and is responsible for the premature death of millions of people worldwide each year. It is closely linked to climate change, affecting air quality and leading to various health issues.

What Can You Do?

  • Reduce energy consumption
  • Use public transportation or carpool
  • Plant trees and support green spaces
  • Support clean energy initiatives

Reduce Emissions

Opt for public transport and reduce car usage to cut down on emissions.

Energy Efficiency

Switch to energy-efficient appliances and lighting to save energy.

Plant Trees

Plant trees in your community to help absorb CO2 and improve air quality.

Support Green Initiatives

Participate in or donate to organizations working towards cleaner air.

Air quality AQI Index Description
Good 0 - 50 Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.
Satisfactory 51 - 100 Air quality is acceptable; however, there may be some pollutants that may pose a moderate health concern.
Moderate 101 - 200 Air quality is acceptable; however, there may be some pollutants that may pose a moderate health concern.
Poor 201 - 300 Air quality is considered unhealthy for sensitive groups, and people with respiratory or heart conditions may experience health effects.
Very Poor 301 - 400 Air quality is considered unhealthy, and everyone may experience health effects; sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
Worst 401 - 500 Air quality is considered hazardous, and everyone may experience more serious health effects.

Good

Description: Air quality is good and air pollution poses no risk.

Who needs concern: Nobody needs to be concerned.

Health effects: No health effects seen.

What to do: It’s a great day to be active outside.

Satisfactory

Description: Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.

Who needs concern: People who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.

Health effects: Possible minor respiratory discomfort for sensitive individuals.

What to do: Consider reducing prolonged or heavy exertion if you are sensitive.

Moderate

Description: Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.

Who needs concern: Sensitive groups.

Health effects: Respiratory symptoms in sensitive individuals, general public unlikely to be affected.

What to do: Reduce outdoor activities if you are sensitive.

Poor

Description: Some members of the general public may experience health effects; members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.

Who needs concern: General public and sensitive groups.

Health effects: Increased likelihood of respiratory symptoms in sensitive groups.

What to do: Limit outdoor exertion, especially for sensitive groups.

Very Poor

Description: Health alert: The risk of health effects is increased for everyone.

Who needs concern: Everyone.

Health effects: Significant increase in respiratory effects.

What to do: Avoid outdoor activities.

Severe

Description: Health warning of emergency conditions: everyone is more likely to be affected.

Who needs concern: Everyone.

Health effects: Serious risk of respiratory effects.

What to do: Stay indoors and limit physical activity.
